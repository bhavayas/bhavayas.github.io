Carrot Shaper - Overview

A mechanical device designed to shape carrots into uniform pieces for food processing. This project focused on precision engineering and food safety standards.

Project Constraints

Table Top Appliance
Food Grade Materials
5 Carrots/min
Seperation of Clean and Dirty Layers

Skills Learned: SolidWorks, CNC Machining, Food Safety Standards, Fatigue Calculations, Contact Force Testing

Carrot Shaper - Design

Brainstormed multiple concepts for carrot shaping mechanism.

Tabletop Track Peeler:
Uses a carrot roller track to rotate carrot while a set of spring supported blades shaves the carrot.
Lathe Based Cutter:
Uses a lathe to rotate carrot while a fixed blade shaves the carrot.
Carrot Shapener:
Uses a conical blade to shave carrot into a circular shape.
Automatic Vertical Peeler:
Uses a vertical blade to shave carrot while a motor moves the carrot through the machine.
Semi-Automatic Lathe Peeler:
Uses a lathe to rotate carrot while a fixed blade shaves the carrot. Operator manually loads and unloads carrot.
Hopper Based Rotational Peeler:
Uses a hopper to hold multiple carrots and a rotating blade to shave the carrots.

Selected the best concept and developed detailed CAD models of each component.

Carrot Holder:
Chosen due to ease of loading multiple carrots and prevent jams.
Carrot Grabber:
Chosen due to ability to hold carrot firmly while shaping.
Peeling Blade:
Chosen due widespread use in food processing, leading to to lower manufacturing costs.
Shaping Blade:
Chosen due to the strength and durability of the blade.
Lead Screw:
Chosen due to ability to provide precise linear motion.
Squeege Table:
Chosen due to ability to collect carrot shavings (clean and dirty) and prevent mess.

something about picking a shaping blade material and overall appliance material

The two main standards followed were the ASME Y14.5 for GD&T and the FDA Food Safety Standards.

Carrot Shaper - Results

The project delivered excellent results in food processing efficiency:

Risk Assessment and Mitigation Strategies (From FMEA Analysis)

  • Contant with Non-Food Safe Materials
    • Mitigation: Isolation of food and non-food zones
  • User harm from moving device white in contact
    • Mitigation: Electrical stop when device door is open
  • Carrot not held with force
    • Mitigation: Spring loaded carrot grabber
  • Carrot not shaped due to dull/broken shaping blade
    • Mitigation: Regular blade replacement schedule

Key Metrics: Consistency rates, processing speed, safety compliance
